Destinations in South America

Antigua, Guatemala


Antigua's beguiling beauty starts to seduce the moment you arrive. Once capital of Guatemala, its streetscapes of pastel facades unfold beneath the gaze of three volcanoes, and beautifully restored colonial buildings sit next to picturesque ruins in park-like surroundings. The city's World Heritage–listed status means that even fast-food chains have to hide themselves behind traditional building facades. There is a lively cultural scene and even its historic sites often host free public events, such as the International Jazz Festival. With many restaurants and bars packed into its centre, the food and drink scene is also one to be savoured.

Buenos Aires, Argentina


A beguiling conflation of European historic outpost and contemporary Latin American sprawl, Buenos Aires was once the capital of one of the world's wealthiest nations. Its French and Italianate buildings, graceful plazas and cultural life are the envy of most Old World cities. All of Buenos Aires is insomniacal – it's so busy reimagining itself, as are its residents, the porteños. The people of Buenos Aires are also distinctive for their accent, which has been strongly influenced by the natural pronunciation and emphasis used in the Italian languages
Become a porteño today, and savour the cultural aroma of Argentina!

Lima, Peru


A fascinating and vibrant city, the Lima you see today is built of ancient Inca temples and monuments, colonial architecture and modern buildings. Lima is the beating heart of modern Peru — a mix of every type of Peruvian, from the coast, the highlands and the jungle — where Peruvian culture, music, food and more have collided to form an urban melting pot that might be hard to love, but is never short on sights, sounds and sensations.

Medellín, Colombia


Situated in a narrow valley, Medellín packs the punch of a city twice its size. Its skyline reaches for the heavens, setting high-rise apartments and office buildings against a backdrop of jagged peaks in every direction. Its pleasant climate gives it its nickname – the City of Eternal Spring – and the moderate temperatures put a spring in the locals' steps, at work and at play. It's a bustling place of industry and commerce, especially in textile manufacturing and exported cut flowers. On weekends Medellín lets its hair down, its many discos attracting the beautiful people.
